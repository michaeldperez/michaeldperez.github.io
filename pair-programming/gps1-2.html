Pair Programming

By Tony Mai and Michael Perez

What is Pair Programming?

A way to avoid creating bugs while programming. A partner helps the other if they get stuck. One person drives/writes the code while the other person navigates.

Why is Pair Programming Important to DBC?

Since there are two people working on it, a pair can divide responsibilities and take advantage of each other's strengths. Gives a chance to solidify knowledge through teaching and learn from your partner.

Compare Driver and Navigator

The drive writes the code and doesn't need to focus on the big picture. The navigator focuses on the big picture and makes sure that the driver is in track.
